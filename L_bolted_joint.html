Step-by-Step Guide with von Mises Stress Calculation

Given:

  • Bolt Size: M12
  • Bolt Grade: 8.8
  • Bolt Material: Steel
  • Preload (Fpre): 40.5 kN
  • Axial Load (Faxial): 10 kN
  • Lateral Force (Flateral): 1 kN
  • Bolt Length (L): 50 mm
  • Distance from Bolt to Force Application Point (e): 50 mm = 0.05 m

Unit Conversion

Convert all measurements to consistent units (meters and Newtons).

  • Bolt Diameter (d): 12 mm = 0.012 m
  • Minor Diameter (dmin): 10 mm = 0.01 m
  • Lateral Force (Flateral): 1 kN = 1000 N

Calculations

1. Preload Stress (\(\sigma_{PL}\)):

\[ \sigma_{PL} = \frac{F_{pre}}{A_t} \] Where \( A_t \) is the tensile stress area. For an M12 bolt, \( A_t \approx 84.3 \, \text{mm}^2 = 84.3 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2 \): \[ \sigma_{PL} = \frac{40.5 \times 10^3 \, \text{N}}{84.3 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2} \] \[ \sigma_{PL} \approx 480.4 \, \text{MPa} \]

2. Axial Stress (\(\sigma_t\)):

\[ \sigma_t = \frac{F_{axial}}{A_t} \] \[ \sigma_t = \frac{10 \times 10^3 \, \text{N}}{84.3 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2} \] \[ \sigma_t \approx 118.6 \, \text{MPa} \]

3. Transverse Shear Stress (\(\tau_{sh}\)):

Using the formula for transverse shear stress: \[ \tau_{sh} = \frac{4}{3} \cdot \frac{F_{\text{lateral}}}{A_t} \] \[ \tau_{sh} = \frac{4}{3} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{N}}{84.3 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2} \] \[ \tau_{sh} = \frac{4}{3} \cdot 11.9 \, \text{MPa} \] \[ \tau_{sh} \approx 15.9 \, \text{MPa} \]

4. Bending Moment

The lateral force also causes a bending moment: \[ M = F_{\text{lateral}} \times e \] \[ M = 1000 \, \text{N} \times 0.05 \, \text{m} \] \[ M = 50 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m} \]

5. Bending Stress (\(\sigma_{bnd}\)):

Using the minor diameter \( d_{min} = 0.01 \, \text{m} \): \[ \sigma_{bnd} = \frac{32 M}{\pi d_{min}^3} \] \[ \sigma_{bnd} = \frac{32 \cdot 50 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}}{\pi (0.01 \, \text{m})^3} \] \[ \sigma_{bnd} = \frac{1600 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{m}}{\pi \cdot 1 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^3} \] \[ \sigma_{bnd} = \frac{1600 \times 10^6 \, \text{N/m}^2}{3.14159} \] \[ \sigma_{bnd} \approx 509 \, \text{MPa} \]

6. Combined von Mises Stress (\(\sigma_{vM}\)):

Using the given formula: \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{[\sigma_{PL} + n(\sigma_t + \sigma_{bnd})]^2 + 3(n \tau_{sh})^2} \] Where \( n \) is a load factor, typically \( n = 1 \). Substitute the values: \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{[480.4 + 1(118.6 + 509)]^2 + 3(1 \cdot 15.9)^2} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{[480.4 + 627.6]^2 + 3(15.9)^2} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{[1108]^2 + 3(15.9)^2} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{[1108]^2 + 3 \cdot 252.81} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{1227664 + 758.43} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} = \sqrt{1228422.43} \] \[ \sigma_{vM} \approx 1108 \, \text{MPa} \]
